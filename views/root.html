What

What

Taking the bus used to be complicated by conflicts between schedules and reality, but in recent years large steps have been taken to allow riders to access real-time arrival data via a variety of platforms. busMSN builds on the premise of using open data to simplify public transit for all, regardless of which platform you choose.

Why

If you want to know how to get from A to B via public transit, which routes arrive at certain stops, or when a bus may arrive, there are myriad options available. However, most people use the same handful of stops every day, and know which buses to take, making directions and a map-based interface irrelevant. With busMSN, stops only need to be selected once before the most relevant data is delivered to you in a single click.

The premise is simple: select the stops you use on a regular basis (for example, work and home) and bookmark the resultant page on all of your devices. When you open busMSN, the closest stop to your current location will be displayed with live bus arrival information.
